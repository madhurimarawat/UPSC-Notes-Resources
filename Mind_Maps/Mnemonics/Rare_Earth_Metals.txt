A rare earth mineral is a natural mineral containing one or more rare earth elements (REEs), which are a group of 17 chemically similar elements (the 15 lanthanides, plus scandium and yttrium) essential for modern technology. Although the name "rare earth" suggests they are scarce, they are actually moderately abundant but challenging to extract because they are found in small, dispersed quantities mixed with other minerals.  [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]  
Key Characteristics 

• Group of 17 elements: The rare earth elements are Scandium, Yttrium, and the 15 Lanthanide series elements. [2, 3]  
• Unique properties: They have unique electronic, magnetic, and optical properties that make them vital for many high-tech applications. [1, 5, 6]  
• Economic value: While not truly scarce, their economic extraction is difficult, making them valuable and sought after. [4, 5]  
• Essential for technology: They are used in everything from cell phones and electric vehicle motors to wind turbines, lasers, and military systems. [3, 5, 6]  

Why they are "rare" 

• Low concentration: Rare earth elements rarely occur in concentrated, economically viable deposits. [4, 5]  
• Complex extraction: The process of separating and purifying these elements from the surrounding ore is complex, energy-intensive, and can be environmentally challenging. [5, 7]
