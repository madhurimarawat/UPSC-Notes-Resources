### ✅ **Anthropology Answer Writing Strategy**

**1. Stick to the Syllabus Keywords 🧾**
UPSC asks direct questions. Use keywords like:

* Ethnocentrism, Cultural Relativism, Neo-evolutionism, Functionalism, Race, Ethnography

**2. Use Thinkers, Theories, and Keywords 👨‍🏫**

* Name-drop essential anthropologists like:

  * **E.B. Tylor**, **Claude Lévi-Strauss**, **Radcliffe-Brown**, **Malinowski**, **Julian Steward**, **Leslie White**
* Mention their **school of thought** (e.g., functionalism, structuralism)
* Always write **“According to...”** to make answers authoritative

**3. Structure Your Answers Well 📦**
Use **Intro – Body – Conclusion (IBC)** format in every answer:

🟨 **Introduction**:

* Define the key concept or quote the definition of an anthropologist.

🟩 **Body**:

* Explain the theory/concept.
* Add **diagrams**, **flowcharts**, and **examples** (tribal examples for Paper II).
* Link with **real-life fieldwork, Indian tribes, case studies**.

🟥 **Conclusion**:

* Add relevance to contemporary society or UPSC values (e.g., inclusive development, sustainable tribal policy)

**4. Use Diagrams & Flowcharts 🧠**

* Evolution of hominids 🦴
* Kinship diagrams 👪
* Tool typology 🛠️
* Tribe location maps 🗺️
  They improve visual appeal and fetch marks.

**5. Indian Anthropology Needs Real Examples 🇮🇳**

* Use real case studies of tribes:

  * Santhals, Gonds, Bhils, Todas, Jarawas
* Mention policies: **PESA, Forest Rights Act, Van Dhan Yojana**

**6. Keep a Comparative Lens 🔍**

* Compare tribes across regions (North-East vs Central India)
* Contrast **Primitive traits vs Modern adaptation**

**7. Focus on Anthropology + Current Affairs 🔄**
Link theory to:

* **COVID-19 and tribal resilience**
* **AI & Evolutionary Biology**
* **Genetic research on race/human origins**
