# 🌍 Formation of Fossil Fuels – Flow Charts

---

## ⚫ 1. Formation of **Coal** (from land plants)

```
🌿 Dense Forests & Swampy Areas
        ↓
🌱 Dead Plants fall into Swamps
        ↓
💧 Buried under Water & Mud
        ↓
❌ No Oxygen (Anaerobic conditions)
        ↓
⏳ Millions of Years
   (High Pressure + High Temperature)
        ↓
🌫️ Peat
        ↓
🪨 Lignite
        ↓
⚫ Bituminous Coal
        ↓
💎 Anthracite Coal (Best Quality)
```

### ✏️ Short Explanation (Coal)

Coal is formed from **dead land plants** buried in **swamps**. Due to **absence of oxygen**, bacteria cannot fully decompose them. Over **millions of years**, **heat and pressure** convert plant remains into different forms of coal.

## 🛢️ Formation of **Petroleum (Crude Oil)** – Scientific Flow Chart

```
🌊 Shallow Seas & Ocean Basins
        ↓
🦠 Dead Marine Micro-organisms
   (Phytoplankton + Zooplankton)
        ↓
⬇️ Deposition on Sea Floor
        ↓
🪨 Burial under Sediments
   (Sand, Clay, Silt)
        ↓
❌ Absence of Oxygen
   (Anaerobic Conditions)
        ↓
🦠 Anaerobic Bacterial Action
        ↓
🧪 Formation of Kerogen
   (Complex Organic Matter)
        ↓
🌡️ Temperature: 60°C – 120°C
   + High Pressure
   (Oil Window)
        ↓
🛢️ Petroleum (Crude Oil)
        ↓
🪨 Migration & Trapping
   (Porous Rocks + Cap Rock)
```

---

### ✏️ Scientific Explanation – Petroleum (Crude Oil)

Petroleum is formed from **dead marine organisms such as phytoplankton and zooplankton** that settled on the sea floor. These remains were buried under sediments in **anaerobic conditions**. Due to **bacterial action**, organic matter first formed **kerogen**. Under **moderate temperature (60–120°C)** and high pressure for **millions of years**, kerogen was converted into **petroleum (crude oil)** and trapped in **sedimentary rocks**.

---

### 🛢️ Petroleum Products (Very Important)

Petroleum is refined by **fractional distillation** into:

* 🛢️ Petrol
* 🚗 Diesel
* ✈️ Kerosene
* 🔥 LPG
* 🧴 Lubricating oil
* 🛣️ Bitumen / Asphalt

## 🔥 Formation of **Natural Gas** – Advanced Scientific Flow Chart

```
🌊 Marine Phytoplankton & Zooplankton
        ↓
⬇️ Burial under Thick Sediments
        ↓
❌ Anaerobic Environment
        ↓
🦠 Anaerobic Bacteria
        ↓
🧪 Kerogen Formation
        ↓
🌡️ Temperature: 120°C – 200°C
   + Very High Pressure
   (Gas Window)
        ↓
🔥 Natural Gas (Methane-rich)
        ↓
📍 Accumulates above Petroleum
   (Lightest Fossil Fuel)
```

---

### ✏️ Scientific Explanation – Natural Gas

Natural gas is formed from the **same marine organisms** as petroleum but at **higher temperatures**. After kerogen formation, when temperature rises to **120–200°C**, kerogen breaks down further to form **natural gas**, mainly **methane (CH₄)**. Because it is **lighter**, natural gas accumulates **above petroleum** in rock layers.

---

## 🔥 Types of Natural Gas

### 1️⃣ **CNG – Compressed Natural Gas**

* Mostly **methane**
* Used as **vehicle fuel**
* Cleanest fossil fuel

### 2️⃣ **PNG – Piped Natural Gas**

* Supplied directly to homes
* Used for **cooking**

### 3️⃣ **LPG – Liquefied Petroleum Gas**

* ❗ Not natural gas
* Obtained from **petroleum refining**
* Contains **propane & butane**

## ✅ Summary

> Petroleum and natural gas are formed from dead marine organisms such as phytoplankton and zooplankton. Due to anaerobic conditions, bacterial action forms kerogen, which on heating at 60–120°C forms petroleum and at 120–200°C forms natural gas.

## 🔽 Sequence of Fossil Fuels (Top to Bottom in Earth)

When fossil fuels are found **underground**, they occur in **layers** based on **density**.

### 🧱 Underground Arrangement (Top → Bottom)

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
🟡 Natural Gas
(lightest – floats at the top)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
🟤 Petroleum (Crude Oil)
(medium density)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
⚫ Coal
(heaviest – found deepest)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

---

## 🧠 Easy Logic to Remember

* **Natural Gas** → lightest → **TOP**
* **Petroleum** → liquid → **MIDDLE**
* **Coal** → solid & heavy → **BOTTOM**

👉 **L–L–H rule**
**Light → Liquid → Heavy**

---

## 📌 Summary

> **In underground deposits, natural gas is found at the top, petroleum below it, and coal at the deepest level.**
