
TRIPS Agreement and India
==========================

1. What is TRIPS?
-----------------
- TRIPS stands for **Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights**.
- It is an international legal agreement administered by the **World Trade Organization (WTO)**.
- Sets **minimum standards** for IP protection and enforcement among WTO members.
- Covers: copyrights, patents, trademarks, geographical indications, trade secrets, industrial designs, integrated circuits, plant varieties.

2. TRIPS and India
-------------------
- India is a **WTO member**, hence bound by TRIPS.
- Came into effect globally in **1995**.
- Developing countries like India received a **transition period** to comply.
- India amended its IP laws:
  - **Patents Act, 1970** → amended in 1999, 2002, 2005 (introduced product patents for pharma & agrochemicals).
  - **Copyright Act** updated.
  - **Trademark Act** updated.
  - **Geographical Indications of Goods (Registration and Protection) Act, 1999** (e.g., Darjeeling Tea).

3. Key Features under TRIPS for India
--------------------------------------
- **Patent term:** 20 years from filing date.
- **Copyright term:** Life of author + 60 years.
- **Compulsory licensing:** Allowed under public health needs (e.g., Natco vs Bayer case).
- **Protection for Traditional Knowledge:** India created **Traditional Knowledge Digital Library (TKDL)** to prevent biopiracy.

4. Important Points for Exams
------------------------------
- TRIPS is part of **WTO agreements** (1995).
- India shifted from **process patents** to **product patents** in 2005.
- TRIPS provides for IP in **trade context**, linking IP with international commerce.
- Issues: balancing IP protection with access to medicines and traditional knowledge.
