📚 Selected UPSC / Prelims Questions on Religion (India) + Answers & Explanations

1. “Souls are not only the property of animal and plant life, but also of rocks, running water and many other natural objects not looked on as living by other religious sects.” (2023 Prelims)
→ Which sect does this reflect?
Answer: Jainism 
Explanation: Jain doctrine teaches that all entities — even inanimate ones like rocks, water, etc. — possess a soul (jīva). This is part of the Jain principle of universal life / sentience.


2. In which one of the following regions was Dhanyakataka, which flourished as a prominent Buddhist center under the Mahasanghikas, located?
(Options: Andhra, Gandhara, Kalinga, Magadha)
Answer: Andhra 
Explanation: Dhanyakataka (also known as Amaravati in Andhra Pradesh) was an important Buddhist site patronized by Mahasanghikas. 


3. The concept of Anuvrata was advocated by (1995 Prelims)
Answer: Jainism 
Explanation: Anuvrata refers to a set of minor vows (restraints) for laity in Jainism—lesser vows compared to the full vows taken by monks.


4. Which of the following statements is/are applicable to Jain doctrine?

1. The surest way of annihilating Karma is to practice penance.


2. Every object, even the smallest particle, has a soul.


3. Karma is the bane of the soul and must be ended.
Options:
a) 1 only
b) 2 and 3 only
c) 1 and 3 only
d) 1, 2 and 3
Answer: d) 1, 2 and 3 
Explanation: Jain doctrine teaches that all three are true: (1) Penance / austerities help in reducing karmic bonds; (2) Every entity has a jīva; (3) Karma is indeed the obstacle to liberation.




5. Which one of the following describes best the concept of Nirvana in Buddhism?
a) The extinction of the flame of desire
b) The complete annihilation of self
c) A state of bliss and rest
d) A mental stage beyond all comprehension
Answer: a) The extinction of the flame of desire 
Explanation: In Buddhist thought, Nirvana is often portrayed as the “extinction” (nirodha) of tanha (craving / desire) and the cessation of suffering, rather than annihilation of self.


6. With reference to the religious history of India, consider the following statements:

1. The concept of Bodhisattva is central to the Hinayana sect of Buddhism.


2. Bodhisattva is a compassionate one on his way to enlightenment.


3. Bodhisattva delays achieving his own salvation to help all sentient beings on their path to it.
Which of the statements given above is/are correct?
Answer: 2 and 3 only 
Explanation:



Statement 1 is false — the Bodhisattva ideal is central to Mahayana Buddhism, not Hinayana.

Statements 2 and 3 properly describe the Mahayana concept of Bodhisattva: compassion and postponement of one's own final nirvana to help others.



7. With reference to the religious practices in India, the “Sthanakavasi” sect belongs to
a) Buddhism
b) Jainism
c) Vaishnavism
d) Shaivism
Answer: b) Jainism 
Explanation: Sthanakavasi is a reform sect within the Svetambara branch of Jainism. They reject idol worship and emphasize inner purity.


8. Which of the following statements about Jainism is/are correct?

1. The most important idea in Jainism is that the entire world is animated; even stones, rocks and water have life.


2. Asceticism and penance are required to free oneself from the cycle of Karma.
Options:
a) 1 only
b) 2 only
c) Both 1 and 2
d) Neither
Answer: c) Both 1 and 2 
Explanation: Both statements correctly reflect core Jain beliefs.




9. With reference to religious history of India, consider the following statements:

1. Sautrantika and Sammitiya were sects of Jainism.


2. Sarvastivadin held that the constituents of phenomena were not wholly momentary, but existed forever in latent form.
Which is/are correct?
Answer: Statement 2 only 
Explanation:



Statement 1 is false: Sautrantika and Sammitiya are Buddhist sects, not Jain sects.

Statement 2 is correct — Sarvāstivādins posited that dharma elements exist in past, present, and future (i.e. a kind of continuity) even though their manifested forms might change.



10. Consider the following statements with respect to Bhakti and Sufi movements:
(a) Chaitanya Mahaprabhu was a proponent of the Achintya Bheda Abheda doctrine and of Gaudiya Vaishnavism.
(b) He was a Nirguna saint.
(c) He strongly propagated devotion (bhakti) to Krishna with attributes.
How many of these statements are correct?
Answer: 2 (i.e. statements a and c) 
Explanation:

Statement (a): correct. Chaitanya propagated the doctrine of Achintya Bheda Abheda (inconceivable oneness & difference) and led the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition.

Statement (b): incorrect, he was not a Nirguna saint; his devotion was to Krishna (a personal deity, Saguna).

Statement (c): correct — his worship was with attributes (form) to Krishna.
