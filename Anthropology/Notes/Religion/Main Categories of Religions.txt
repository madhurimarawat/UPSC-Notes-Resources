1️⃣ Main Categories of Religions

Religions are often classified by how they view the divine (Gods/spirits):

Monotheism → Belief in one God.
Examples: Judaism, Christianity, Islam, Sikhism, Zoroastrianism.

Polytheism → Belief in many gods/deities.
Examples: Hinduism, Shinto, Ancient Greek/Egyptian religions.

Henotheism → Belief in one supreme God but acceptance of others.
Example: Some schools of Hinduism (worship of one deity like Vishnu or Shiva but acceptance of others).

Animism → Belief that spirits or divine forces exist in nature (trees, rivers, animals, mountains).
Example: African tribal religions, Indigenous religions, Native American traditions.

Atheistic / Non-theistic → Religions that do not emphasize God/gods, but focus on philosophy, ethics, or self-liberation.
Example: Buddhism, Jainism, Confucianism (though some followers add ritual elements).
