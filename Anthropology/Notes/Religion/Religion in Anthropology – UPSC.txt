📘 Religion in Anthropology – UPSC Optional Notes


---

1. Introduction

Religion is a universal cultural phenomenon found in all human societies.

Anthropology studies origin, function, and evolution of religion.

It connects with belief systems, rituals, magic, myths, social order, and symbols.



---

2. Anthropological Approaches to Religion

Thinker              | Approach                        | Key Ideas & Examples
---------------------|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------
E.B. Tylor (1871)    | Evolutionary (Animism)          | Religion originates in belief in souls/spirits → animism → polytheism → monotheism → science. 
                     |                                 | Examples: Andamanese tribes 🌿, Ancient Egyptians 🏺

James Frazer (1890)   | Magic → Religion → Science      | "Golden Bough" – magic fails → people turn to religion → later replaced by science. 
                     |                                 | Examples: Trobriand magic 🎣, Ancient Roman rituals 🏛️

Durkheim (1912)       | Functionalist                   | Religion unites people; distinction between sacred & profane; rituals strengthen social solidarity. 
                     |                                 | Examples: Australian Aboriginal ceremonies 🔥, Hindu festivals 🕉️

Malinowski           | Psychological Functionalism      | Religion & magic help reduce anxiety and uncertainty. 
                     |                                 | Examples: Trobriand fishing magic 🎣, Rainmaking rituals 🌧️

Radcliffe-Brown      | Structural-Functionalist        | Rituals maintain social structure and continuity. 
                     |                                 | Examples: African tribal rites 🛖, Japanese Shinto festivals ⛩️

Clifford Geertz      | Symbolic/Interpretive           | Religion = cultural system of symbols giving meaning to life. 
                     |                                 | Examples: Balinese Hinduism 🌺, Catholic symbolism ✝️

3. Key Concepts

Magic: Individual attempt to control supernatural (e.g., Trobriand Islanders).

Religion: Collective rituals and beliefs in supernatural beings.

Science: Empirical, rational explanation of natural phenomena.

Myths: Sacred stories explaining origins, legitimizing authority.

Rituals: Symbolic acts (rites of passage, seasonal, fertility rituals).

Totemism (Durkheim): Animals/plants as sacred symbols of clans.

Mana (Codrington): Impersonal supernatural power.

Shamanism: Religious specialist mediating with spirits.

4. Typology of Religions (Anthropological Classification)

Type                            | Definition                                           | Example
--------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------
Animism                         | Belief in spirits of nature or ancestors           | Tribes (Andamanese, Australian Aboriginals)
Animatism                       | Belief in impersonal supernatural force (mana)    | Polynesian cultures
Totemism                        | Sacred relationship between a group and a natural object | Australian tribes, Ojibwa
Polytheism                      | Belief in multiple gods                             | Ancient Greek religion, Hinduism
Monotheism                      | Belief in one God                                   | Islam, Christianity
Monism / Philosophical Religion | Belief in ultimate reality as one                   | Vedanta, Buddhism (nirvana concept)

5. Religion in Indian Society (Paper II Focus)

a. Hinduism

Varna and caste justified by religious texts (Rigveda, Manusmriti).

Karma, Dharma, Moksha – guiding principles.

Strong ritual and symbolic traditions (samskaras, festivals).


b. Buddhism & Jainism

Rejected Vedic ritualism, focused on ethical conduct.

Egalitarian → challenged caste order.

Spread across Asia (Buddhism more successful).


c. Bhakti and Sufi Movements

Promoted devotion, equality, and syncretism.

Kabir, Nanak, Chishti saints.

Weakened caste barriers and promoted interfaith harmony.


d. Tribal Religions

Animism, animatism, ancestor worship.

Integration with Hinduism/Christianity but retain distinct rituals (e.g., Sarna, Donyi-Polo).


e. Modern Context

Secularism (Constitution Articles 25–28).

Reform movements: Arya Samaj, Brahmo Samaj, Ambedkarite Buddhism.

Syncretism → pluralism is India’s strength.
