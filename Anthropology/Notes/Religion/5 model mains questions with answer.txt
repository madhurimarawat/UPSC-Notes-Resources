Paper I (Social-Cultural Anthropology)

Q1. Discuss the anthropological approaches to the study of religion, highlighting the views of E.B. Tylor, Emile Durkheim, and Clifford Geertz. (20 marks)

Answer (outline):

Tylor (Animism): Religion originates from belief in spirits → basis of all religions is animism.

Durkheim (Sacred & Profane): Religion is a social phenomenon; unites community through rituals and collective effervescence.

Geertz (Symbolic/Interpretive): Religion is a cultural system of symbols giving meaning to existence.

Comparison: Evolutionary (Tylor) → Functionalist (Durkheim) → Symbolic (Geertz).

Anthropology now studies religion in context (not only origins).



---

Q2. Differentiate between Magic, Religion, and Science with anthropological examples. (15 marks)

Answer (outline):

Magic: Individual, manipulative, based on belief in cause-effect (Malinowski’s Trobriand Islanders → magic used in open sea fishing).

Religion: Collective, ritualistic, based on faith in supernatural (Durkheim – sacred/profane).

Science: Empirical, experimental, rational.

They coexist: In tribal societies, magic complements religion; in modern societies, science challenges but doesn’t fully replace belief.



---

Q3. Examine the role of rituals and myths in sustaining social order. (15 marks)

Answer (outline):

Myths: Narratives of origin, gods, ancestors → legitimizes authority, morality (e.g., creation myths in tribes).

Rituals: Repetitive practices reinforcing solidarity (rites of passage: birth, marriage, death).

Durkheim: Rituals strengthen collective conscience.

Example: Hindu rituals (samskaras), Christian mass, tribal fertility rituals → all ensure continuity and stability.

Paper II (Indian Anthropology)

Q4. Discuss the impact of religion on caste system in India. (20 marks)

Answer (outline):

Hindu religion & Varna system: Religious sanction (Purusha Sukta of Rigveda) gave birth to caste hierarchy.

Dharma & Karma theory: Justified caste inequality.

Buddhism, Jainism, Bhakti, Sufi: Challenged caste rigidity, promoted equality.

Modern scenario: Secularism + reform movements (Arya Samaj, Ambedkarite Buddhism).

Religion both entrenched caste and inspired reform.



---

Q5. Write short notes on Religious Syncretism in Indian society. (10 marks)

Answer (outline):

Syncretism = blending of traditions and practices of different religions.

Examples:

Bhakti–Sufi traditions (Kabir, Nanak emphasized unity of God).

Tribal-Hindu integration (Adivasi deities merged with Hindu pantheon).

Christianity in Kerala adopting local rituals.


Importance: Shows Indian pluralism and accommodation.
