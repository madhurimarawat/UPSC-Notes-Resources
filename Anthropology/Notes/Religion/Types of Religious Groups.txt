Types of Religious Groups:

---

1. Cult 🌀

* Definition: A small, new, or unorthodox religious group, often led by a charismatic leader.
* Key Feature: Often seen as outside mainstream society.
* Example: Heaven’s Gate (US) 🚀

---

2. Sect ✝️☪️✡️

* Definition: A subgroup that breaks away from a larger religious tradition due to differences in beliefs or practices.
* Key Feature: Maintains most of the parent religion’s core beliefs but has distinctive practices.
* Example: Amish (from Christianity) 🌾

---

3. Denomination ⛪🕌🕍

* Definition: A recognized, organized branch of a major religion with its own institutions and practices.
* Key Feature: Larger, formalized, part of mainstream religion.
* Example: Roman Catholicism (Christianity) ✝️, Sunni Islam ☪️, Reform Judaism ✡️

---

4. Church ⛪

* Definition: A large, established religious organization with a formal structure and community of followers.
* Key Feature: Considered mainstream; often politically and socially influential.
* Example: Church of England 🇬🇧

---

5. New Religious Movement (NRM) 🌱

* Definition: A recently founded religion or spiritual group, often with innovative beliefs or practices.
* Key Feature: Can start as a cult and grow into a denomination over time.
* Example: Scientology 👽, Bahá’í Faith 🌈

---

6. Congregation 🙏

* Definition: A local group of worshippers who meet regularly in a place of worship.
* Key Feature: Can belong to a denomination or church; focuses on community worship.
* Example: A local parish, mosque, or synagogue 🕌⛪🕍
